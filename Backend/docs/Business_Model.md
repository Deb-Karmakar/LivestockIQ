# Business Model - LivestockIQ

> Sustainable revenue strategy for a public health digital platform addressing antimicrobial resistance in India's livestock sector.

## Executive Summary

LivestockIQ operates on a **"Free for farmers, value for stakeholders, funded by ecosystem"** model. The platform remains free for farmers while generating revenue through government contracts, veterinarian subscriptions, and industry partnerships.

---

## Core Philosophy

| Principle | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Farmers always free** | Low-income users; adoption is priority over revenue |
| **Government as anchor customer** | SIH origin; align with NAP-AMR budget |
| **Vets as revenue multipliers** | Pay for professional tools; onboard farmers |
| **Industry pays for compliance** | Dairy/meat need verified safe products |
| **Data creates moat** | Unique AMU dataset = competitive advantage |

---

## Revenue Streams

### 1. Government Contracts (B2G) — Primary Revenue (60%)

| Service | Revenue Model | Estimated Value |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| State-level deployment | Annual license per state | ₹50L - 2Cr/state/year |
| National AMR Monitoring Dashboard | SaaS to DoAH&D | ₹5-10 Cr/year |
| Integration with NADCP/NADRS | One-time + maintenance | ₹1-2 Cr + annual |
| Customization for state schemes | Project-based | ₹25-50L per project |
| Training & capacity building | Per batch | ₹2-5L per district |

**Target Clients:**
- Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry & Dairying (DoAH&D)
- State Animal Husbandry Departments
- National Dairy Development Board (NDDB)
- FSSAI (for food safety monitoring)

---

### 2. Veterinarian Premium Plans (B2B) — Secondary Revenue (15%)

| Plan | Price | Features | Target |
|------|-------|----------|--------|
| **Free** | ₹0 | 20 farmers, 100 animals, basic prescriptions | New vets |
| **Professional** | ₹499/month | 100 farmers, 500 animals, analytics, priority support | Private practitioners |
| **Enterprise** | ₹1,999/month | Unlimited, multi-clinic, API, white-label | Vet chains, NGOs |

**Conversion target:** 10% of registered vets on paid plans by Year 2

---

### 3. Dairy & Meat Industry (B2B) — High-Value Revenue (20%)

| Customer | Service | Revenue Model |
|----------|---------|---------------|
| **Dairy Cooperatives** | MRL compliance verification for milk procurement | ₹0.50 - ₹1 per verification |
| **Meat Processing Plants** | Withdrawal period verification for slaughter | ₹2-5 per animal clearance |
| **Export Houses** | Compliance certification for EU/US exports | ₹500-2000 per certificate |
| **Retail Chains** | "Antibiotic-Free" verification badge | Annual partnership fee |

**Example Revenue Calculation:**
```
Amul daily procurement: 3.6 million farmers
Even ₹0.10 per compliance check = ₹36 lakh/day potential
```

**Target Partners:**
- Amul, Mother Dairy, NDDB member cooperatives
- Al Kabeer, Allana, Hind Agro (meat exporters)
- BigBasket, Zepto, Blinkit (retail)

---

### 4. Pharmaceutical Companies (B2B) — Data Revenue (3%)

| Service | Revenue Model | Value |
|---------|---------------|-------|
| Anonymized drug usage analytics | Annual subscription | ₹10-50L/year per pharma |
| AMU benchmarking reports | Quarterly reports | ₹5-10L/quarter |
| New drug launch targeting | Lead generation | ₹1000 per qualified lead |
| Pharmacovigilance data | Adverse event reporting | Per-report fee |

**Compliance:** All data anonymized and aggregated; no individual farmer data sold.

**Target Clients:**
- Zoetis, MSD Animal Health, Elanco
- Indian pharma: Vetoquinol, Indian Immunologicals, Hester Bio

---

### 5. Farmer Value-Added Services (B2C) — Upsell Revenue (2%)

| Service | Price | Description |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| Insurance Integration | Commission | Partner with livestock insurers |
| Input Marketplace | 5-10% commission | Veterinary medicines, feed, supplements |
| Premium AI Tips | ₹99/month | Advanced health predictions |
| Compliance Certificates | ₹50-100/cert | Downloadable PDF for premium buyers |
| Training Programs | ₹200-500 | Online courses on animal health |

## Summary: Why This Model Works

1. **Aligns with public health mandate** — AMR is a national priority
2. **Farmers stay free** — Ensures adoption without financial barriers
3. **Multiple revenue streams** — Reduces dependency on single source
4. **Network effects** — More users = more data = more value
5. **Regulatory tailwinds** — Government likely to mandate compliance tracking
6. **Impact + Profit** — Rare combination attractive to impact investors
